Temporary Measure to Validate Your Identity

If you are unable to validate your identity through the CRA, you will need to validate it with a third party. The validator must be 18 years of age or older and must have known you personally for at least 2 years. The validator can be a relative.

The validator must:

  1. Confirm that the photo on the identification document (ID) is a true likeness of the Primary Officer and that the expiration date on the document is valid;
    • Acceptable IDs are:
      • A valid passport; or
      • 2 government-issued IDs (one document must include a photo), such as a:
        • Provincial or territorial driver’s licence
        • Provincial or territorial health card
        • Provincial or territorial birth certificate
        • Provincial or territorial identification card (a photo card with the same security features as a driver’s licence)
        • Permanent resident card
        • Record of landing
        • Canadian citizenship certificate
        • Certificate of registration of birth abroad
        • Certificate of Indian Status issued by the Government of Canada
        • Refugee Protection Claimant Document
        • Work permit
  2. Write a clear and legible letter that includes the:
    • date the validation took place;
    • first name, last name and User Reference Number of the ROE Web user who wants to become the Primary Officer;
    • following attestation: “I hereby certify that I have validated the identification of the user with a valid passport or 2 pieces of valid identification, including one piece that contained a photo, and I certify it to be a true likeness of the user”;
    • signature, name, official position and title of the person who certified the document.

    3. For privacy purposes, the validator is not required to send copies of the documents used to authenticate and validate your identity to Service Canada.

  3. Submit a scanned copy of the signed letter and submit by email to Service Canada: NC-ROE-EMPLOYER-SERVICES-EMPLOYEURS-RE-GD@servicecanada.gc.ca

Once you successfully validate your identity, Service Canada will email you an authorization code using the email address in your ROE Web profile. You must enter this code in your profile to complete the registration.

A letter will also be mailed to the owner or CEO of the organization to inform them of the ROE Web changes.
